The Vanishing Archive

AR Escape Room TECH 295 Spring 2026 Group Project

The Vanishing Archive is an augmented reality escape room game where players take on the role of archival investigators trying to recover a set of historic documents that have mysteriously disappeared from a university library. Players must solve a series of narrative-driven puzzles to uncover clues, decode messages, and ultimately locate the missing collection before time runs out.

The game is played using a mobile app that guides players through an introductory video setting the scene, then presents three interconnected puzzles. Each solved puzzle reveals more of the story. Players who complete all puzzles discover the identity of the person who hid the documents and why, earning the "win" outcome. Those who fail receive a cryptic final message hinting at what they missed.

My role: Lead game designer and puzzle architect. I designed the overall narrative structure, created the flowchart connecting story beats to puzzle outcomes, wrote the Game Design Document (GDD), and designed two of the three puzzles. I also served as game master during playtesting sessions.

Tidebreak

Board Game Personal Project 2025

Tidebreak is a cooperative tabletop board game for 2-4 players set in a fictional coastal village threatened by rising sea levels. Players work together as village council members to build defenses, relocate residents, and manage limited resources across 10 rounds. The game uses a deck-building mechanic combined with area-control elements on a modular hex board.

Design goals: I wanted to explore environmental themes through cooperative mechanics, where players must negotiate and prioritize as a group. The game went through 12 iterations of playtesting with groups ranging from casual players to experienced board gamers.
